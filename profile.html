It's well known that data science is deeply rooted in the disciplines of statistics and computer science. Less appreciated are the influences and underpinnings of the scientific method. At its core, data science conducts an investigation through experimentation, hypothesis testing, and reason to better understand the natural world. In a sense, statistics and computer science are simply the tools of a data scientist to help them conduct an investigation.

Undergraduate research in seismology and geodynamics introduced me querying and cleaning messy seismogram datasets of large proportions. I applied mathematical transformations, created geospatial models, and visualized datasets in creative ways to draw conclusions to present to the wider scientific community. I continued conduct similar research at the United States Geological Survey, where I maintained, cleaned, and transforming large datasets, created statistical models, and visualized data in a quest for understanding.

Wanting to become a better scientist, I've since devoted myself to studying how to best utilize the invaluable tools of statistical analysis and computer programming. While I still identify with and enjoy earth science, somewhere on this journey I've fallen more in love with Data Science.
